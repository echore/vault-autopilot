# Video Keyframe (Motion) Analysis SOP

_After reading this document, output your analysis directly in the framework below._

> Input: a set of keyframe screenshots in time order. The images sit
> right above the analysis. What a single frame looks like, people can
> see for themselves. The analysis only covers what you can see across
> frames: how the segment moves from start to end, and what makes that
> flow good.

---

## Output Structure

**Why It Works** (2-4 bullets)
Each bullet points at one design move that spans frames. Only write
what a single frame cannot show: order, rhythm, pauses, hiding and
revealing, dimming and highlighting, how the camera moves.
Format:
- **[The move]**: why it works, one sentence. To copy: one actionable sentence.

**My Thoughts**
(leave blank, the human fills this in)

---

## Hard Rules

- Describe no single frame. What is visible in one image, skip; write
  only what appears across images.
- Do not guess. No subtitles means do not invent what is being said;
  subtitles burned into the frame are fine to quote, that counts as
  pointing at things.
- Point at things. Every bullet must say which frames it happens
  between.
- The generic test: if a bullet still holds under a different motion
  sequence, it is empty talk.
- Plain speech. Short sentences, one idea per sentence. If you would
  not say it out loud, rewrite it.
